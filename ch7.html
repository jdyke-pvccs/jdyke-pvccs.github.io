Briefly describe or define
  • Computer network: a set of independent computer systems interconected by telecommunication links for the purpose of sharing information and resources
    		•
  • Analog device: combinations of both analog machine and analog media that can together measure, record, and brodcast continuous information
    		•
  • Digital device: electronic device that contains a computer or a microcontroller that processes, stores, and sends data in the form of digital signals by two states in 1s and 0s
    		•
  • Modem: performs the inverse operation, which is called demodulation. It takes the received waveform, separates the carrier wave from the encoded digital signal, and passes on the digital data to the computer.
    		•
  • Bandwidth: rate at which information could be sent and received.
    		•
  • WLAN: a short-distance form of wireless networking
    		•
  • IEEE 802.11 wireless network standard: Wi-Fi is used to connect a computer to the Internet when it is within range of a wireless base station
    		•
  • MAN: wireless network whose scope is larger than the few hundred feet of a WLAN, typically, a few blocks up to entire city.
    		•
  • Internet of Things: objects that can be controlled by other devices on the same network with a radio frequency identifier (RFID)
    		•
  • LAN: connects hardware devices such as computer, printers, and storage devices that are all in relatively close proximity.
    		•
  • Repeater: a device that simply amplifies and forwards a signal.
    		•
  • Bridge: a "smarter" device that has knowledge about the nodes located on each separate network
    		•
  • WAN: connects devices that are not in close phsical proimity.
    		•
  • Packet and packet switching: information block with fixed maximum size that is transmitted through the network as a single unit.
    		•
  • ISP: a business whose purpose is to provide access from a private netowrk (such as a corprate or university network) to the individual's home computer to the internet.
    		•
  • Firewall: hardware and software placed at the entry points to a company's private network to protect it from unauthorized access.
    		•
  • Protocol: a mutually agreed-upon set of rules, conventions, and agreements for the efficient and orderly exchange of information.
    		•
  • Cound Computing: behaves much like the client/server and client nodes that access those services. However, with cloud computing the servers no longer need to be local to the client population, and they no longer mush be provided by your own organization

    Briefly describe the five laters of TCP/IP (the Internet protocol hierarchy):
  • Layer 1. Physical layer: govern the exchange of binary digits (bits) across a physical communication channel, such as a fiber-optic cable, copper wire, or wireless radio channel.
    		•
  • Layer 2. Data link layer: address and solve two issues-error handling and framing. This process is done in two stages called Layer 2a, Medium Access Control, and Layer 2b, Logical Link Control.
    		•
  • Layer 3. Network layer: delivers a message from the site where it was created to its ultimate destination.
    		•
  • Layer 4. Transport layer: assigns port numbers to programs and remembering which program goes with which port.
    		•
  • Layer 5. Application layer: rules for implementing the end-user services provided by a network, and they are built on top of the four protocol layer.
    		•
